Pocket UIKit

The Pocket UIKIt is a collection of styled and functional HTML elements you can use to build the Pocket component of you Hoodie plugin. Its aim is to make your life easier by reducing the amount of CSS and JS you need to deal with, and it aims to make our users' experience better by providing a consistent experience across plugins written by different authors

We don't impose the UIKit on you, so feel free to build crazy stuff on top of or even entirely without it.

Including the UIKit

The UIKit consists of one CSS and one JS file that you need to include in your plugin's pocket/index.html. These are served to you from the hoodie-server, so they're only available when your Hoodie app is running.

In your html's head: 

<link rel="stylesheet" href="/_api/_plugins/_assets/styles/pocket-uikit.css">

And before the closing body tag:

<script src="/_api/_plugins/_assets/scripts/pocket-uikit.js"></script>

Quickstart Template

Here's a handy template so you can get started without any hassle:

<!doctype html>
<html lang="en">
<head>
  <meta charset="UTF-8">
  <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">
  <title>My Hoodie Plugin</title>

  <!-- Pocket UIKit CSS -->
  <link rel="stylesheet" href="/_api/_plugins/_assets/styles/pocket-uikit.css">
</head>
<body>
  <div class="container">
    <!-- This is where all your stuff goes -->
  </div>

  <!-- Pocket UIKit JS (includes jQuery and Boostrap Components) -->
  <script src="/_api/_plugins/_assets/scripts/pocket-uikit.js"></script>
</body>
</html>

Please put all of your plugin's markup in the div with the class container.

Headlines

Pocket's UIKit offers a couple of headline styles, where h1 is also used as a section separator with a horizontal border at the bottom.
